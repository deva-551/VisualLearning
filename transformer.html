The Big Picture

AI used to read through a
tiny keyhole.

Before 2017, AI read text one word at a time, left to right. It forgot earlier words by the time it reached the end.

The Transformer changed everything by letting AI see the entire sentence at once and understand how every word relates to every other word. This is why modern AI can write essays, answer questions, and hold conversations.

What does GPT stand for?

Uses the Transformer architecture — the engine under the hood.

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — they all use a Transformer to understand your words and generate a response, one word at a time.

Why Transformers Matter

The "Bank" Problem

Old AI gave every word one fixed meaning. But words change meaning based on context.

🌳🌊
"bank"
Context: river
Meaning: edge of a river
vs
🏦💰
"bank"
Context: money
Meaning: financial institution

Static embeddings: one fixed meaning — always wrong for one context.
Contextual attention: reads surrounding words and adapts — gets it right every time.

Self-Attention in Action

Who is "it"?

Self-attention is the brain of the Transformer — it processes entire sentences at once, understanding word relationships.

The animal didn't cross the street because it was tired.
Attention links "it" to "animal", not "street" — because being tired is a property of living things. The Transformer computes attention scores between every pair of words to figure this out.

This is exactly what happens in the demo — every word looks at every other word and asks: "How relevant are you to my meaning?"
