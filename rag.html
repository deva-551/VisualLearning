The Problem

LLMs are smart.
But they have blind spots.

📅

Knowledge Cutoff

Trained on old data. Doesn't know what happened yesterday.

👻

Hallucination

Makes up confident answers when it doesn't actually know.

🔒

No Private Data

Can't see your company docs, databases, or internal files.

The Solution

Give the LLM
an open-book exam.

RAG retrieves your real documents and feeds them to the LLM at question time. No retraining needed. No guessing. Just accurate, grounded answers.

But first — the document needs to be broken down into searchable pieces.

Step 1

Split the document into
focused chunks.

A whole document is too big and too vague to search.
Small chunks give precise, findable pieces.

📄 Annual_Report_2024.pdf

Revenue $142M... employees 2,400... AI R&D $50M... satisfaction 94%... TechFest $2.8M...

✂
Q3 revenue $142M
2,400 employees
$50M AI R&D
94% satisfaction
TechFest $2.8M

Each chunk covers one idea. Searching for "Q3 revenue" will find exactly that chunk — not a vague blob of the whole document.

Step 2

Each chunk gets
a number identity.

An embedding model reads each chunk and converts it into a list of numbers — called a vector. Chunks with similar meaning get similar numbers, so they land close together.

The embedding model is a small AI trained purely to understand meaning. It doesn't answer questions — it just turns text into coordinates. All vectors are then stored in a vector database, ready to be searched in milliseconds.

Step 3

Find the closest chunks
to answer your question.

Your question is also run through the embedding model to get its vector. Then the database finds the chunks whose vectors are nearest — those are the most relevant ones.

Closer = more relevant. The top matching chunks are pulled out and passed to the LLM as context — so it can give a grounded, accurate answer instead of guessing.

The Result

Same question.
Completely different answer.

❌ Without RAG

👤 "What was Q3 revenue?"
🧠 LLM guesses from memory...
👻 "Revenue was likely around $80-90M..."Completely fabricated. Real answer is $142M.

✅ With RAG

👤 "What was Q3 revenue?"
🔍 Retrieves: "Q3 revenue reached $142M"
🧠 LLM reads the real document
✅ "Q3 revenue was $142M, a 23% YoY increase."Accurate. Grounded in real data.
